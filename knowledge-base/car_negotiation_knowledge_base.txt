## SITUATION 1: "YOU NEED TO COME TO THE DEALERSHIP"

### What Dealer Says:
- "For pricing, you need to come into the dealership"
- "You would have to come in and negotiate"
- "You have more leverage face to face"
- "We don't discuss pricing over the phone"

### How to Respond:

**First, ask about car availability:**
"Has the car arrived at the store yet?"

**If YES (car is there):**
"Well, my client's already driven one at another dealer and fell in love with it. They're ready to close the deal today if the numbers work. So there's really no need for us to come in‚Äîwe just need to see the final price and we can move forward."

**If NO (car hasn't arrived):**
"If the car's not there yet, what's the point of coming in? Let's get the numbers sorted out first, and once the car arrives and the price works, we can schedule a time to come by."

**Alternative responses:**
- "I already drove one and loved it. I just need the numbers to see if we can make a deal."
- "I already know I want it. I don't need to come in. Just need your best out-the-door price."
- "I've driven it already. Really just trying to get the best price."

### Why This Works:
- Eliminates their test drive objection
- Shows you're a serious buyer
- Forces them to compete on price alone
- Removes their "get you in the door" leverage

## SITUATION 2: "WE DON'T GIVE PRICES OVER THE PHONE"

### What Dealer Says:
- "Typically we don't give out numbers over the phone"
- "We can't provide pricing without you coming in"
- "I can't give you the best number over the phone"

### How to Respond:
**Response A:** "Can you email it to me instead? If the numbers work, I'll come in today."

**Response B:** "I'm calling several dealerships. Just need your best price to compare."

**Response C:** "Just text me the out-the-door price. If it works, this is a done deal."

### Why This Works:
- Offers alternative to phone (email/text)
- Creates competition pressure
- Shows serious intent with conditions
- Gets written documentation

## SITUATION 2A: REACTING TO PRICING

### When You Hear Their Price:

**If the price is good/competitive (matches or beats your competitive price):**
- "I love it! That's exactly what I was hoping for!"
- "Beautiful! That's a winner right there."
- "Awesome! My client's gonna be stoked."
- "Bada boom, bada bing! Can you email that to me?"
- "Perfect! That works. Can you send that breakdown to quotes@carmommy.compare?"
- **DO NOT keep negotiating if the price is already good**

**If there are add-ons included in the price:**
- "Okay, so you're saying it's [price] with [add-ons]?"
- "Gotcha. What's the price if we remove those add-ons?"
- Then go to SITUATION 5 for removing add-ons

**If it's clearly too high:**
- "I mean, I appreciate that, but that doesn't really wake me up and get me excited, you know?"
- "Ooof, that's a bit higher than I was hoping for."
- "What? That's crazy! I was thinking more like [lower number]."

**If price is at MSRP (and you don't have competitive pricing):**
- "I appreciate that, but I'm really looking for the best deal possible. Can you do better than MSRP?"
- "I hear you, but I know there's usually some room to work with. What can you do?"

**If price is above MSRP:**
- "That's above MSRP‚Äîcan you do better than that?"
- "No way! That's above sticker. What's going on there?"

**If you need them to improve:**
- "I'm looking for a better deal than that. What can you do?"
- "What kind of discount can you offer? I know there's usually some room to work with."

### Why This Works:
- React naturally and enthusiastically to good prices
- Show genuine emotion (builds rapport)
- Express disappointment on bad deals without being rude
- Give them a chance to improve their offer

## SITUATION 3: LARGE DEPOSIT DEMANDS ($2,000+)

### What Dealer Says:
- "We need a $5,000 deposit"
- "All these cars have deposits on them"
- "Someone else is putting a deposit down"
- "We need deposit to hold the car"

### How to Respond:
**Response A:** "I'll give you $1,000 if we agree on numbers first."

**Response B:** "Have they completed it yet?" (if claiming someone else has deposit)

**Response C:** "Send me the price breakdown first, then we can talk about deposit."

**Response D:** "I can deposit today if the numbers work. What's your best out-the-door price?"

### Why This Works:
- Counter-offers reasonable amount
- Calls their bluff on other buyers
- Makes deposit conditional on price
- Shows you're serious but not desperate

## SITUATION 4: MARKUP / ABOVE MSRP PRICING

### What Dealer Says:
- "We're at MSRP" (but actually above)
- "There's a market adjustment"
- "We have additional markup"
- "That's just how the market is right now"

### How to Respond:
**Response A:** "Right now you're above MSRP. I need MSRP or better to make this work."

**Response B:** "I know the MSRP is $X. Your price is above that. Can you do MSRP?"

**Response C:** "Other dealerships are selling at MSRP. Why should I pay more here?"

**Response D:** "I'm looking for MSRP or below. What can you do?"

### Why This Works:
- Shows you know the actual MSRP
- Creates price anchor at MSRP
- References competition
- Establishes clear walk-away point

## SITUATION 5: MANDATORY DEALER ADD-ONS

### What Dealer Says:
- "All our vehicles come with a protection package"
- "This has ceramic coating/window tint already"
- "$6,000 in add-ons included"
- "These add-ons are already installed"

### How to Respond:
**Response A:** "I don't want the add-ons. What's the price without them?"

**Response B:** "Can you remove the protection package? I don't need it."

**Response C:** "I'm not paying for add-ons I didn't request. Remove them from the price."

**Response D:** "I say no to those add-ons. What's the price without the clear shield and protection package?"

### Why This Works:
- Directly challenges "mandatory" claim
- Shows you know they're negotiable
- Reduces dealer profit significantly
- Most aren't actually installed yet

## SITUATION 7: ASKING FOR PERSONAL INFORMATION TOO EARLY

### What Dealer Says:
- "Let me get your information first"
- "What's your address?"
- "I need to make a customer profile for you"
- "What's your phone number and email?"

### How to Respond:
**Response A:** "Let's agree on numbers first, then we can exchange information."

**Response B:** "My information doesn't change the price of the car. What's your best price?"

**Response C (playful):** "I don't give my address to people I just met. Stranger danger! Let's talk price first."

### Why This Works:
- Maintains privacy until deal is close
- Prevents dealer from running credit
- Keeps focus on pricing
- Light humor builds rapport

### What Dealer Says:

- Turns urgency back on them
- Requests verification

- "It'll be somewhere in that range"

- Enables real comparison
- Shows you're detail-oriented

## SITUATION 12: FINANCING / PAYMENT FOCUS

### What Dealer Says:
- "What monthly payment are you looking for?"
- "How much do you want to pay per month?"
- "Let's work backwards from your budget"
- "We can get you into this car for $X/month"

### How to Respond:
**Response A:** "I'm focused on the out-the-door price first. We can discuss financing after."

**Response B:** "What's the total price of the car? That's what I need to know."

**Response C:** "I need the OTD price. I'll handle my own financing."

### Why This Works:
- Prevents monthly payment manipulation
- Keeps focus on total cost
- Dealer can't extend loan term to hide price
- Shows financial sophistication

## SITUATION 13: TRADE-IN PRESSURE

### What Dealer Says:
- "Do you have a trade-in?"
- "What are you driving now?"
- "We can give you great value on your trade"

**Response C:** "Not trading in. What's your best out-the-door price."

### Why This Works:
- Prevents bundled negotiation
- Dealer can't hide discount in trade
- Keeps negotiations separate
- Maintains clarity

## SITUATION 15: COMPETING DEALER MENTION

### When You Have a Better Quote from Another Dealer:

**How to Use It:**
"I have another dealer at $X out-the-door. Can you beat that?"

**Or:**
"I'm comparing several dealerships. Your price is $X. Other dealer is at $Y. Can you match or beat it?"

**When to Use:**
- After getting their first quote
- When they claim "best price"
- To create urgency
- To force their final offer

**Why This Works:**
- Creates real competition
- Gives them specific target
- Shows you're shopping around
- Motivates their best price

## IDEAL CALL SEQUENCE

1. **Opening:** "I already drove one and loved it. I'm calling about [specific car]."

5. **Get specific:** "What's the exact OTD price including all fees and taxes?"

6. **Document:** "Can you email that breakdown to [email]?"

## KEY PRINCIPLES

1. **Always lead with "I already drove it"** - Eliminates test drive objection
2. **Focus on out-the-door price** - Prevents fee surprises
3. **Get everything in writing** - Email or text confirmation
4. **Challenge all add-ons** - They're almost always optional
5. **Use conditional commitment** - "If we agree on [price], I'll..."
6. **Create competition** - "I'm comparing several dealers"
7. **Be specific** - Use exact numbers, not ranges
8. **Stay friendly but firm** - Build rapport while holding ground
9. **Don't accept first no** - Push back politely
10. **Walk away if needed** - Your strongest power
11. **Be genuinely human** - Use natural reactions, humor, and enthusiasm
